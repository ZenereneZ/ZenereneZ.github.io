Electrical Design

Tinah

reneZ uses a Tinah board as its microcontroller. The Tinah is similar to an arduino, but was created for the ENPH 253 class to provide more functionality for our robot. Some of the features include:

  • -4 DC Motor outputs
  • -3 Servo outputs
  • -2 buttons and 2 knobs for on board control
  • -LCD
  • -More can be found here

H-bridge

The motor outputs on the Tinah board can only provide a motor 9V and 1A. We used Geared Barber Coleman motors which needs ~12V and 1.5A. In order to get enough power to these motors we needed to make a separate H-bridge. A H-bridge is used in order to control the speed and direction of a DC motor, essential for driving our robot.

IR-Detector

For our competition surface, our robot needed to detect 1kHz and 10kHz infrared frequencies in order to complete the track.
